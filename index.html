How it reconciles

The three stages a transaction passes through

Every transaction is checked against three sequential stages: funds entering the wallet, being reserved & moved out, then arriving at the partner. A transaction is fully reconciled only when all three line up.

01
Deposit
deposit sheet

Merchant funds the wallet. A pair of legs: dr on the bank account, cr on the merchant wallet. Reversals appear as uppercase CR/DR.

02
Parking
parking sheet

Funds reserved for a txn, then released to the partner. Two sub-steps:

2a Confirm — biz.merchant.pmnt.confirm — earmarks the funds against a Confirm Reference.
2b Dispatch — biz.mar.pmnt.dp — pushes funds out, tied to a Dispatched Reference.
03
Dispatched
Dispatched sheet

Partner-side legs. We sum the dr and cr entries for each Dispatched Reference — they should net to zero, confirming the payout settled cleanly.

Reconciled — all three stages match the txn and balance.
Amount mismatch / Unbalanced — present but values disagree.
Missing — one or more stages absent. See Exceptions tab.

Deposits vs transactions

Variance = Deposited − Gross collection
Deposited is net: original cr deposits minus uppercase-DR reversals on the wallet. Each deposit is then matched to txns up to seven ways — single txn, batch sum, multi-batch, multi-txn, mixed (batches + standalone txns combined), FX match (cross-currency, rate within workbook range), or likely duplicate (same amount, same merchant, within 7 days, more deposits than txns). A single deposit that funds many txns is the common case for merchants like Global Money Express. The deeper question is which specific deposits remain unaccounted for — see the Unmatched deposits tab.
≈ 0 Every deposit accounted for via a txn, batch, or subset-sum. Fully closed.
Positive Unspent wallet balance OR currency mismatch — check unmatched deposits.
Negative Spending exceeds deposits — txn(s) without backing funds. Investigate.

BOK FX1014 / FX1015 · USD conversion check

Verifybefore you submit.

Upload a BOK FX1014 (outbound) or FX1015 (inbound) per-transaction report and this re-runs the Bank of Korea USD-conversion checks locally — the same rules that produced the 28 J-errors + 5 T-errors on the 2026-06-30 filing. The report type is detected automatically and every row BOK would reject is flagged before you send the file.

What it checks

  • Each report carries two USD-converted amounts; each must equal amount ÷ rate, be positive and rounded to 2 decimals.
  • FX1014: J (송금USD환산금액) = PAYOUT AMOUNT ÷ USD V LCY RATE; T (송금의뢰USD환산금액) = COLLECTION AMOUNT ÷ USD V COLLECTION RATE.
  • FX1015: J (송금USD환산금액) = COLLECTION AMOUNT ÷ COLLECTION USD RATE; S (송금수취USD환산금액) = PAYOUT AMOUNT ÷ PAYOUT USD RATE.
  • Primary side rejected > 20% off, secondary > 25% off; rate = 0 rows are flagged as open items.
  • Data starts at row 3 (row 1 = English headers, row 2 = Korean headers). Nothing is uploaded — all processing runs in your browser.

USD conversion & field-length checks FX1014

Column J (송금USD환산금액) must equal PAYOUT AMOUNT ÷ USD V LCY RATE, positive and rounded to 2 decimals. BOK rejects any row deviating more than 20% from that value, or any negative value.

Column T (송금의뢰USD환산금액) must equal COLLECTION AMOUNT ÷ USD V COLLECTION RATE, positive and rounded to 2 decimals. Tolerance is looser than J — rejected around 25% off.

Rate = 0 (USD V LCY RATE I = 0, or USD V COLLECTION RATE S = 0) makes the formula undefined. These are open items — enter a real rate or use the opposite-side value. Do not invent a value.

Each BOK FX1015 field has a fixed maximum length (속성/길이). Any value that exceeds it is rejected or silently truncated by BOK. Text fields count characters; dates count digits (YYYYMMDD = 8); amounts count digits plus the decimal point. Most commonly BENEFICIARY ID NUMBER (field 19, X(15)) overflows when a BZ prefix + 14-digit account number = 16 characters.

Within tolerance now (accepted) but more than 2% off the exact formula — systematic drift that could tip over the rejection line next cycle. Worth watching, no action required.
